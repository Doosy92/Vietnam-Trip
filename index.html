1
Arrival Day — Settle In
Thursday 11 June
Travel Food Easy
▼

  • Check into Lotus Saigon Hotel, freshen up, and leave luggage
  • Gentle evening stroll near the hotel — District 3 has great street food
  • Try a bowl of Pho Bo or Banh Mi from a nearby local spot

🚖 Travel: Airport → Lotus Saigon Hotel. Use Grab app (~40 min, ~150,000 VND). Do NOT use unofficial taxis at the airport.
2
History & Markets
Friday 12 June
History Shopping Food
▼
  • 7:30 AM — War Remnants Museum (opens 7:30). Go early to beat heat & crowds. Has a lift — great for Dad. Emotionally powerful — allow 2–3 hours.
  • 11:00 AM — Grab back to hotel for lunch break and pool time. Rest during the hottest part of the day.
  • 2:00 PM — Ben Thanh Market for afternoon browse. Great for souvenirs, coffee, silk scarves, dried goods. Haggling is expected and fun!
  • Evening — Street food dinner around Ben Thanh Night Market area
🚖 Travel: Hotel → War Remnants Museum (~5 min Grab, very close). Museum → Hotel → Ben Thanh (~10 min Grab). Walk between all nearby.
💡 Tip: At Ben Thanh, start by offering 25–30% of the asking price and meet in the middle. It's part of the experience — be cheerful about it!
3
Cu Chi Tunnels Day Trip
Saturday 13 June
History Outdoors
▼
  • 7:30–8:00 AM — Tour bus pickup from hotel (book in advance via Klook or GetYourGuide)
  • ~1.5–2 hr drive northwest of HCMC through the countryside
  • Watch the historical documentary film at the site (air-conditioned) — great for Dad
  • See outdoor exhibits: jungle traps, bomb craters, military equipment on display
  • Jason can crawl through the famous tunnels — Dad can watch from above!
  • Lunch at the site or en route back
  • 3–4 PM — Return to hotel. Rest and rooftop pool evening
🚌 Travel: ~1.5–2 hrs each way by tour bus from hotel. Full tour ~6–7 hrs total. Book a small group tour for comfort (max 8–12 people recommended).
💡 Dad tip: There's a lift, benches, and shade throughout. The tunnels themselves are optional — there's plenty to see above ground.
4
Mekong Delta Boat Tour
Sunday 14 June
Nature Food Relaxed
▼
  • 7:30–8:00 AM — Tour pickup from hotel
  • ~1.5–2 hr drive south to My Tho / Ben Tre entry point
  • Board a river boat — cruise the Mekong's channels and islands
  • Visit coconut candy workshop, bee farm with honey tea tasting, tropical fruit garden
  • Traditional folk music performance while eating fresh fruit
  • Narrow canal canoe ride through the jungle (rowed by locals — just sit back!)
  • Lunch included on tour
  • 5–6 PM — Return to hotel. Last night in HCMC — dinner out!
🚌 Travel: ~1.5–2 hrs each way. Full day tour ~8–10 hrs. Very low effort — mostly seated on boats or in air-conditioned bus. Perfect for Dad.

6
Hanoi Old Quarter & Hoan Kiem Lake
Tuesday 16 June
History Food Shopping
▼
  • Morning — Slow start after late arrival. Breakfast at a local café. Try Bun Cha (grilled pork noodles) — a Hanoi classic.
  • 9:00 AM — Hoa Lo Prison (the "Hanoi Hilton"). Fascinating French colonial-era history. Audio guide highly recommended (~1.5 hrs).
  • 11:00 AM — Walk to Hoan Kiem Lake (10 min). Beautiful flat lake circuit. Visit Ngoc Son Temple on the island. Perfect for photos.
  • Afternoon — Wander the Old Quarter's 36 ancient streets. Each street once sold a different trade — silk, paper, tin, herbs. Great browsing.
  • Evening — Street food walk. Try egg coffee, Banh Mi, and fresh spring rolls.
🚶 Travel: All within walking distance of each other in the Old Quarter. Flat terrain. Grab available if Dad needs a rest.
7–8
Ha Long Bay — 1 Night Cruise ⭐
Wednesday–Thursday 17–18 June
Nature Food Relaxed
▼
  • Day 7 — 8:00 AM — Coach pickup from hotel. Scenic 3.5 hr drive to Ha Long Bay.
  • ~12:00 PM — Board your cruise ship. Welcome lunch on board as you sail into the bay.
  • Afternoon: cruise through towering limestone karst scenery, cave visits (Sung Sot Cave is spectacular), kayaking optional.
  • Sunset on the deck with drinks. Seafood dinner on board.
  • Sleep on the boat overnight — the rocking is lovely!
  • Day 8 — Morning — Sunrise on deck, morning activities, breakfast on board. Disembark ~12:00 PM.
  • ~3:30–4:00 PM — Coach back to Hanoi. Arrive hotel ~7:30 PM.
🚌 Travel: 3.5 hrs each way by coach. Book a mid-range cruise with cabin options for 4 — look at Heritage Line, Indochine Cruise, or similar. Book ASAP — June sells out fast!
💡 Dad tip: Almost entirely on the boat — very low exertion. Kayaking is optional. The main cave has some steps but guides assist. This is the highlight of the whole trip.

Recovery Day — Temple of Literature & Shopping
Friday 19 June
Culture Shopping Easy
▼

  • 9:30 AM — Temple of Literature: Vietnam's first university (1070 AD). Beautiful courtyards, peaceful atmosphere. Hire a guide for context. ~1.5 hrs.
  • Afternoon — Dedicated shopping in the Old Quarter. Hang Gai Street for silk, lacquerware and custom clothing. Dong Xuan Market for a local market feel.

Be Prepared

Packing & Tips

👕
Clothing
  • Light, breathable fabrics only (linen, cotton)
  • Modest cover-ups for temples (shoulders & knees)
  • Rain jacket or packable poncho (June = rainy season)
  • Comfortable walking sandals + sneakers
  • Swimwear (rooftop pool + Ha Long Bay)
  • Light layer for air-conditioned restaurants & buses
💊
Health & Medical
  • Consult doctor 6–8 weeks before — Hep A, Typhoid, Tetanus
  • Mosquito repellent (DEET-based, 50%+)
  • Sunscreen SPF50+ (reapply constantly in June heat)
  • Rehydration sachets — humidity is intense
  • Basic first aid kit + any personal medications
  • Travel insurance with medical cover — essential!
📱
Tech & Money
  • Download Grab app before you arrive (like Uber)
  • Get a local SIM at the airport — cheap and fast
  • Notify your bank before travel
  • VND cash for markets and street food
  • Portable phone charger / power bank
  • Universal adaptor (Vietnam uses Type A/C)
🎒
Day Trip Essentials
  • Small daypack for excursions
  • Reusable water bottle (stay hydrated!)
  • Wet wipes & hand sanitiser
  • Hat / cap for outdoor sites
  • Waterproof bag for Ha Long Bay boat
  • Snacks for long bus transfers

Essential Tips

🇦🇺 Visa

Australians currently receive 45 days visa-free entry to Vietnam. No visa needed — just your passport, which must be valid for 6+ months from your travel dates.

🌧️ June Weather

June is early rainy season — expect afternoon showers, especially in the south. Temperatures are 28–35°C. Rain passes quickly. Carry a poncho and embrace it!

🚖 Getting Around

Use Grab (the local Uber) for all taxis — safe, reliable, and metered. Never use unofficial taxis at airports. Haggle at markets but not with Grab.

🍜 Food Safety

Street food is generally safe and delicious — eat where locals eat. Avoid ice in drinks unless at a proper restaurant. Don't drink tap water. Bottled water everywhere.

📅 Book in Advance

Ha Long Bay cruises sell out fast in June. Book as early as possible. Cu Chi Tunnels and Mekong Delta tours can be booked 1–2 days ahead via Klook or GetYourGuide.

💰 Costs

Vietnam is very affordable. Street meals: ~30–80k VND. Restaurant mains: ~100–200k VND. Grab rides in the city: ~50–150k VND. Budget roughly AUD $80–120/day per person.
