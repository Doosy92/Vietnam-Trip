Day by Day

The Plan

I've tried to get the balance right, with enough to see the good stuff without completely exhausting ourselves. Plenty of boat rides, Grab taxis and relaxed afternoons built in. Click each day to expand it.

🏙️
Ho Chi Minh City
11-15 June · 4 nights · Lotus Saigon Hotel, District 3
11 Jun
Arrival Day
Thursday 11 June
TravelFoodEasy
▼
  • Land at 4:15pm. It's about 45 minutes by Grab from Tan Son Nhat Airport to our hotel in District 3, so we'll be checked in by around 5:15pm. Freshen up and head out for dinner.
  • The hotel rooftop pool is on level 19 if anyone's feeling more awake than expected.
Use the Grab app for taxis, not the random ones at the airport. Set it up before we land.
12 Jun
History & Markets
Friday 12 June
HistoryShoppingFood
▼
  • Early start for the War Remnants Museum. It's about a 10-minute Grab from the hotel, or 20 minutes on foot if the morning is cool enough. Worth going before the heat kicks in. It's heavy going emotionally. Take your time with it.
  • Back to the hotel by midday for lunch and a rest. Then Ben Thanh Market in the afternoon. It's about a 5-minute Grab from the hotel, or walkable in around 15 minutes. Good for souvenirs, coffee, scarves, snacks.
  • The market area turns into a street food night market after 6pm, which is a great way to spend the evening.
Haggling is expected at Ben Thanh. Start low and just walk away if they won't budge. Works every time.
13 Jun
Cu Chi Tunnels Day Trip
Saturday 13 June
HistoryOutdoors
▼
  • Tour bus picks us up in the morning. The drive is about 1.5 hours northwest of the city, so we'll be there by mid-morning. I'll book a small group one rather than the giant 40-person buses. It's a really fascinating piece of history and a completely different side of Vietnam.
  • Jason, you can crawl through the actual tunnels if you want to. I'd maybe go with the enlarged tourist version rather than the original ones, unless you're keen to really test yourself 😂
  • Back at the hotel by mid-afternoon (another 1.5 hrs back). Rooftop pool. You'll have earnt it.
Everything is at your own pace, with plenty of shade and benches, no rushing.
14 Jun
Mekong Delta: Full Day Boat Tour
Sunday 14 June
NatureFoodRelaxed
▼
  • Full day out on the Mekong. The coach picks us up from the hotel and it's about 1.5 hours south of HCMC to reach the delta. Canals, rice paddies, coconut palms, small fishing villages. It's a completely different side of Vietnam to the city.
  • Stops along the way at a coconut candy factory (Mum, this one's for you), a bee farm, and a tropical fruit garden with live traditional music. Lunch is included.
  • There's a narrow canal canoe ride where local rowers take you through the jungle. You just sit back and take it in.
  • Last night in HCMC when we get back, so let's find somewhere good for dinner.
15 Jun
Free Morning · Fly to Hanoi
Monday 15 June
RelaxTravel
▼
  • Nothing planned for the morning. Sleep in, rooftop pool one last time, leisurely breakfast.
  • Head to the airport in the afternoon. Tan Son Nhat is about 40 minutes from District 3, so aim to leave by 5:30pm for our 8:30pm departure. The flight is 2 hours 5 minutes and lands at 10:35pm. Transfer will be pre-booked so it's straight to the hotel and bed. Noi Bai Airport is about 45 minutes from the Old Quarter.
🏯
Hanoi
15-23 June · 7 nights · Old Quarter (hotel TBC)
16 Jun
Old Quarter, Hoan Kiem Lake & Hoa Lo Prison
Tuesday 16 June
HistoryFoodShopping
▼
  • Slow start given we arrived late. Hanoi feels completely different to HCMC. Older, cooler, more French colonial. Worth just taking it in over breakfast.
  • Hoa Lo Prison when we're ready. It's about a 10-minute walk from most Old Quarter hotels, or a very short Grab. It covers both the French colonial era and the Vietnam War and it's fascinating. Get the audio guide, it's worth it.
  • Walk down to Hoan Kiem Lake after. It's about 10 minutes on foot from Hoa Lo Prison. There's a little temple on an island in the middle connected by a red bridge which is really beautiful. Everything today is pretty much walkable.
  • Afternoon wander through the Old Quarter's 36 streets (you could walk end to end in 20 minutes, but you won't), then street food in the evening. Try the egg coffee.
17-18 Jun
Ha Long Bay: Mon Cheri Cruise ⭐
Wednesday-Thursday 17-18 June
NatureFoodUnmissable
▼
  • Ha Long Bay. Thousands of limestone towers rising out of emerald green water. It's quite something.
  • Coach picks us up from the hotel around 8am for the drive to the bay. It's about 3.5 hours to Ha Long City, so we arrive at the harbour around 11:30am, board the Mon Cheri, and have lunch on deck as we sail out. The moment the limestone karsts appear around you, everyone goes quiet. It does that to people.
  • Afternoon cruising, a cave visit, optional kayaking. Sunset on deck with drinks, seafood dinner on board, then sleep on the boat.
  • Sunrise on deck the next morning, breakfast on board, then the 3.5-hour coach back to Hanoi. We'll be back in the Old Quarter by early evening.
Kayaking is completely optional. You can just sit on the deck and let the views do the work.
19 Jun
Temple of Literature & Shopping
Friday 19 June
CultureShoppingEasy
▼
  • Slow start after two days on the bay. No alarm, proper breakfast.
  • Temple of Literature when we're ready. It's about a 15-minute Grab from the Old Quarter. Vietnam's first university, built in 1070. The courtyards are beautiful and it's a peaceful spot. Worth hiring a guide there as the history is what makes it.
  • Afternoon shopping in the Old Quarter. Hang Gai Street is walkable from the hotel, and Dong Xuan Market is about 15 minutes on foot through the old streets.
  • For dinner, try Cha Ca La Vong. Turmeric fish cooked at the table with dill. Worth trying.
20 Jun
Ninh Binh: Ha Long Bay on Land
Saturday 20 June
NatureHistoryScenic
▼
  • Early start for this one. Worth it, I promise.
  • Ninh Binh is about 2 hours south of Hanoi by car, so we'll aim to leave around 7:30am and be there by 9:30am. It's called the inland Ha Long Bay and that's exactly what it looks like. Same dramatic limestone karsts, but surrounded by rice paddies and rivers instead of sea. It's stunning in a completely different way.
  • At Trang An, local rowers take us through caves, past ancient temples and under limestone cliffs. The boat trip itself is about 2.5 hours. You just sit there and take it in.
  • Lunch in town. Local speciality is goat meat. Don't knock it until you've tried it.
  • The 2-hour drive back means we'll be in Hanoi by early evening for a quiet night.
21 Jun
Chill Day & Street Food Tour
Sunday 21 June
FoodRelaxed
▼
  • Morning coffee by Hoan Kiem Lake. It's about 10 minutes on foot from the Old Quarter hotels. Locals do tai chi here and it's a lovely atmosphere. Just sit and watch the city wake up.
  • Afternoon is free. Mum, I'd strongly recommend a spa. Vietnamese massage is exceptional and it's very affordable. Jason can find his own trouble 😄
  • Evening street food tour of the Old Quarter. About 3 hours. Banh Mi, egg coffee, spring rolls, and a Vietnamese sweet dessert called Che. Go in with an open mind on that last one.
Egg coffee at Cafe Giang on Hang Gai Street. Whipped egg yolk over espresso. Tastes like liquid tiramisu. Do not skip it.
22 Jun
Flex Day
Monday 22 June
FoodShoppingFree
▼
  • I've left the last full day open on purpose. By this point we'll know what we feel like.
  • My vote is a cooking class. Learn to make Pho, Banh Mi and spring rolls and actually go home able to cook the food we've been eating all trip.
  • Other options: the Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum if anyone's curious (about 20 minutes by Grab from the Old Quarter), last minute shopping in the Old Quarter, or a spa day. All equally valid.
23 Jun
Departure Day
Tuesday 23 June
Travel
▼
  • Last morning. One final walk around Hoan Kiem Lake if anyone's up for it. It's a 10-minute stroll from the hotel.
  • Checkout at 7:30am. Noi Bai Airport is about 45 minutes from the Old Quarter, which is why we're leaving that early. Flight home at 9:55am. Check in closes 90 minutes before, so there's no room for a lie-in on this one.
  • Back in Melbourne by 10:15pm. Then immediately start planning when we can go back.
Noi Bai Airport is 45 minutes away. The 7:30am checkout is not negotiable.
