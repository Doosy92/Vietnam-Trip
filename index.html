Full Itinerary

I've kept the pace relaxed throughout — nothing too hectic, and I've made sure Dad won't be walking marathons anywhere. Tap each day to see what's planned.

Ho Chi Minh City
11 – 15 June · 4 nights · Lotus Saigon Hotel, District 3
1
Arrival — We Made It! 🎉
Thursday 11 June
TravelFoodEasy
▼
  • Land at 4:15 PM — open Grab and order a taxi to the hotel. Do NOT get in any unmarked taxis at the airport exit, they'll rip you off. Grab only.
  • Check in to the Lotus Saigon Hotel, drop the bags, and go straight up to the 19th floor rooftop pool. First drink of the trip up there — non-negotiable.
  • Easy evening. Gentle walk around District 3, grab some pho from one of the street spots nearby. Nothing fancy for night one.
  • Early night — we'll have been travelling since 10:35 AM Melbourne time so everyone will be flagging by 9 PM.
🚖 Getting there: Grab from the airport to the hotel — about 40 mins, roughly 150,000 VND (around $10 AUD). Make sure everyone has Grab downloaded before we land.
2
War Museum & Ben Thanh Market
Friday 12 June
HistoryShoppingFood
▼
  • 7:30 AM — War Remnants Museum. Go early before the heat hits and the crowds build up. There's a lift so no stairs for Dad. Fair warning — it's emotionally heavy going, but it's one of the most important things you can do here. Allow 2–3 hours and don't rush it.
  • 11:00 AM — Back to the hotel for lunch and a swim. Midday heat in June is no joke — this is the right call.
  • 2:00 PM — Ben Thanh Market. Great for souvenirs, coffee, silk scarves and dried goods. Haggling is expected — don't be shy about it, that's the whole point. Start by offering about 25% of what they ask.
  • Evening — Wander the Ben Thanh night market area for dinner. Street food stalls all around.
🚖 Getting around: Hotel to the museum is literally 5 mins in a Grab. Ben Thanh is another 10 mins from there. Everything's very close.
💡 Chris's tip: At the markets, smile, be cheerful about it, and don't take the haggling personally — they enjoy the back-and-forth as much as you do. Start at 25% and meet them somewhere in the middle.
3
Cu Chi Tunnels Day Trip
Saturday 13 June
HistoryOutdoors
▼
  • 7:30–8:00 AM — Tour bus picks us up from the hotel. I'll book a small group tour in advance (max 8–12 people) — much better than a giant tourist bus.
  • About 1.5–2 hour drive northwest out of the city through the countryside. Bring snacks for the bus.
  • They start with an air-conditioned room showing a documentary film — really good, and perfect for Dad. Have a seat and take it all in.
  • Then outdoor exhibits all around — jungle traps, bomb craters, military gear. Genuinely fascinating.
  • Jason — you can crawl through the actual tunnels if you're up for it. They're tiny and hot. I'd still recommend it. Dad gets to stand above and watch.
  • 3–4 PM — Back at the hotel. Rooftop pool and cold drinks. You'll have earned it.
🚌 Travel time: ~1.5–2 hrs each way. Full day is around 6–7 hours. Small group tour is worth the extra cost — look on Klook or GetYourGuide.
💡 For Dad: Genuinely no pressure to do anything strenuous. There are benches and shade throughout. The documentary and outdoor exhibits are the main event — the tunnel crawling is purely optional.
4
Mekong Delta Boat Tour
Sunday 14 June
NatureFoodRelaxed
▼
  • 7:30–8:00 AM — Tour bus pickup from the hotel.
  • About 1.5–2 hours south to where we board the river boats.
  • From here it's almost entirely on boats — we cruise through the channels and stop at a coconut candy workshop, a bee farm with honey tea tasting, and a tropical fruit garden. Really lovely stuff.
  • There's a folk music performance while they feed us fresh fruit. Sounds a bit touristy but it's genuinely a nice moment — Mum, you'll love this bit.
  • Then a canoe ride through a narrow jungle canal — the locals do all the rowing, we just sit there and enjoy it.
  • Lunch is included in the tour.
  • 5–6 PM — Back in the city. Last night in HCMC — let's go somewhere decent for dinner.
🚌 Travel time: ~1.5–2 hrs each way. Full day is 8–10 hours but almost all of it is sitting on boats or the bus. This one is the easiest day of the whole HCMC leg — great for Dad.
5
Free Morning · Fly to Hanoi
Monday 15 June
RelaxTravel
▼

  • Afternoon — Check out, store bags with reception. Last wander or any final shopping.
  • ~6:00 PM — Head to SGN Airport Terminal 3. Allow at least 2 hours.
  • 8:30 PM — Fly to Hanoi. Quick 2-hour hop.
  • 10:35 PM — Land in Hanoi. Airport transfer already booked — straight to the hotel. Bed immediately.

Hanoi
15 – 23 June · 7 nights · Old Quarter (hotel TBC)
6
Old Quarter, Hoan Kiem Lake & Hoa Lo Prison
Tuesday 16 June
HistoryFoodShopping
▼
  • Slow morning — We arrived late last night so no early starts. Breakfast at a local café. Order Bun Cha — it's Hanoi's signature dish, grilled pork noodles, and it's incredible.
  • 9:00 AM — Hoa Lo Prison (the famous "Hanoi Hilton"). Really gripping colonial-era history. Worth getting the audio guide — makes a massive difference.
  • 11:00 AM — 10-minute walk to Hoan Kiem Lake. Beautiful, flat, easy for everyone. Visit the little temple on the island in the middle. Great for photos.
  • Afternoon — Wander the Old Quarter streets. These have been selling the same things for hundreds of years — silk on one street, paper on the next. Great for a browse and some shopping.
  • Evening — Street food walk. Order egg coffee — it sounds weird but trust me on this one, it's one of the best things I've ever tasted.
🚶 Getting around: Everything here is within walking distance of the Old Quarter. Completely flat terrain. Grab available anytime if anyone needs a rest.
7–8
Ha Long Bay — 1 Night Cruise ⭐
Wednesday–Thursday 17–18 June
NatureFoodRelaxed
▼
  • Day 7 — 8:00 AM — Coach picks us up from the hotel. 3.5 hour drive to the bay — genuinely beautiful countryside along the way.
  • ~12:00 PM — Board the cruise ship. Welcome lunch as we sail out. The first moment you see the limestone karsts rising out of the water, you'll understand why everyone raves about this place. It's extraordinary.
  • Afternoon — we'll visit Sung Sot Cave (enormous, spectacular), kayaking is on offer if anyone wants it, sunset drinks on deck, and a proper seafood dinner on board.
  • Sleep on the boat. The gentle rocking is genuinely great for sleeping.
  • Day 8 — Morning — Sunrise from the deck if you're up for it. More time on the bay, breakfast on board. Disembark around midday.
  • ~3:30 PM — Coach back to Hanoi, arriving around 7:30 PM. Quiet evening.
🚌 Travel: 3.5 hrs each way. I'm looking at Heritage Line or Indochine Cruise for a mid-range option with proper cabins. This needs to be booked well in advance — June fills up fast and I'm on it.
💡 For Dad: This is genuinely the most relaxed day of the whole trip. You're on the boat, the food comes to you, and the scenery does all the work. The cave has a few steps but the guides help everyone through at their own pace.
9
Temple of Literature & Shopping Day
Friday 19 June
CultureShoppingEasy
▼
  • Late start — Recovery morning after Ha Long Bay. Breakfast at the hotel, take your time.
  • 9:30 AM — Temple of Literature. Vietnam's very first university, built in 1070 AD. The courtyards are stunning and it's really peaceful — nothing like the chaos of the streets outside. Hire a guide, it's worth every dong.
  • Afternoon — Dedicated shopping. Hang Gai Street for silk and lacquerware. Custom tailoring is available with 24-hour turnaround if anyone wants something made. Dong Xuan Market for a more local feel.
  • Evening — Try Cha Ca La Vong — turmeric fish cooked at your table. It's a Hanoi institution and genuinely delicious.
🚖 Travel: About 15–20 mins by Grab to the Temple of Literature. Back to the Old Quarter is another 15 mins.
10
Ninh Binh — Ha Long Bay on Land
Saturday 20 June
NatureHistoryRelaxed
▼
  • 7:00 AM — Early start, sorry! Coach from the hotel. Bring snacks.
  • ~2.5 hour drive south through the Red River Delta.
  • Trang An — this is what everyone calls "Ha Long Bay on land". Same towering limestone mountains but surrounded by rice paddies, and you explore it by rowboat. The locals do all the rowing — we just sit back.
  • Hoa Lu Ancient Capital — Vietnam's very first royal capital. Now completely surrounded by mountains. Really atmospheric place.
  • Lunch in Ninh Binh. The local speciality is goat meat — I'd encourage everyone to give it a try.
  • ~3:30 PM — Drive back to Hanoi.
  • ~6:30 PM — Back at the hotel. Easy evening, early night.
🚌 Travel: ~2.5 hrs each way. Long day (~11 hours total) but the rowboat through the karst mountains is one of those things that photos genuinely don't do justice. Worth every minute of the drive.
11
Chill Day & Evening Street Food Tour
Sunday 21 June
FoodRelaxed
▼
  • Morning — Coffee by Hoan Kiem Lake. The locals do tai chi around it every morning and it's a lovely thing to watch. Very peaceful start to the day.
  • Afternoon — I'd strongly recommend a spa afternoon. Vietnamese traditional massage is incredible and it costs almost nothing compared to home. Mum — you especially, you'll thank me later.
  • 5:00 PM — Evening street food walking tour of the Old Quarter. About 3 hours at a relaxed pace. We'll try Bun Bo, Banh Mi, egg coffee, fresh spring rolls, and Che (a sweet dessert soup). You don't have to finish everything — just try a bit of each.
💡 Don't skip the egg coffee. Cafe Giang on Hang Gai Street is where it was invented. Sit upstairs, look out over the street, and order one. One of those proper travel moments you'll remember.

From Chris

What to Pack & Things to Know

A few things I want to make sure everyone knows before we go. Please read this properly — especially the health bit. Mum, I'm looking at you.

👕
Clothes
  • Light, breathable fabrics only — linen or cotton. It is HOT and humid in June. Leave the jeans at home, seriously.
  • Bring a scarf or something to cover shoulders and knees for temples — they won't let you in without it.
  • A light rain jacket or cheap poncho — June is rainy season. Showers are sudden but they pass quickly.
  • Comfortable sandals AND sneakers. We'll do some walking but nothing crazy.
  • Swimwear — rooftop pool in HCMC and Ha Long Bay.
  • One thin layer for air-conditioned restaurants and buses — they absolutely blast the AC.
💊
Health Stuff
  • See your GP at least 6–8 weeks before we go. Ask about Hep A, Typhoid and Tetanus. I've already sorted mine.
  • Good mosquito repellent — DEET-based, 50%+. Don't go cheap on this one.
  • SPF50+ sunscreen. Reapply constantly. The June sun will get you faster than you expect.
  • Rehydration sachets — the humidity will knock you around the first couple of days.
  • Any regular medications — bring enough for the full trip plus a few extra days just in case.
  • Travel insurance with proper medical cover. Please sort this before we go.
📱
Tech & Money
  • Download the Grab app before we land — it's like Uber and we'll use it constantly.
  • Grab a local SIM at the airport — very cheap, and you'll want data for maps and Grab.
  • Let your bank know you're travelling so they don't block your card.
  • Bring some VND cash — markets and street food stalls are often cash only.
  • A portable phone charger — full days out will drain your battery.
  • Universal adaptor (Vietnam uses Type A and C plugs).
🎒
Day Trip Bag
  • A small backpack for the day trips — nothing too big.
  • Reusable water bottle. Stay hydrated, it's easy to forget when you're busy.
  • Wet wipes and hand sanitiser — essential for market and street food days.
  • A hat or cap for outdoor sites. Not optional in June.
  • A waterproof bag for Ha Long Bay — the kayaking gets wet.
  • Snacks for the long bus rides (Cu Chi and Ninh Binh especially).

A Few Important Things

🇦🇺 No Visa Needed

Great news — Australians get 45 days in Vietnam completely visa-free. All you need is a passport valid for at least 6 months from when we travel. Check yours now if you haven't already.

🌧️ June Weather

It'll be around 28–35°C and very humid, with afternoon showers most days. The rain is warm and passes fast — honestly it's quite refreshing after the heat. Just carry the poncho and don't stress about it.

🚖 Always Use Grab

Never get in an unmarked taxi — always use the Grab app. It's metered, safe, and cheap. This especially applies at airport arrivals where dodgy drivers will approach you directly. Walk straight past them and open Grab.

🍜 Street Food is Fine

Eat where locals eat — busy stalls mean fresh food and fast turnover. Avoid ice in drinks unless you're at a proper restaurant. Never drink the tap water, but bottled water is everywhere and costs almost nothing.

📅 Ha Long Bay — Book Early

This is the one thing that needs to be sorted well in advance. June is peak season and the decent boats fill up months ahead. It's top of my list and I'm on it — will update here once it's confirmed.

💰 What Things Cost

Vietnam is genuinely very affordable. Street food is around $2–5 AUD a meal. Restaurant mains are $7–15 AUD. Grab rides around the city are $3–10 AUD. Budget about $80–120 AUD per person per day and you'll be comfortable with room to spare.
