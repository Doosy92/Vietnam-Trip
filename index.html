Day 1 · District 3, HCMC
02
History & Markets
Friday 12 June
HistoryShoppingFood
▼
  • 7:30 AM — War Remnants Museum. Go early to beat the heat and crowds. Emotionally powerful but genuinely unmissable — one of the most important things to see in Vietnam. Allow 2–3 hours.

Day 2 · HCMC Sights
03
Cu Chi Tunnels Day Trip
Saturday 13 June
HistoryOutdoors
▼
  • 7:30–8:00 AM — Tour bus pickup from hotel. Book in advance via Klook or GetYourGuide. Small group tours (8–12 people) are far better than big buses.
  • ~1.5–2 hr drive northwest through the countryside.
  • Air-conditioned film screening at the site — great historical context before the outdoor exhibits.
  • Outdoor exhibits: jungle traps, bomb craters, military equipment. Benches and shade throughout, completely at your own pace.
  • The tunnels themselves are optional — there's a huge amount to see above ground.
  • 3–4 PM — Return to hotel. Rooftop pool evening — that 19th floor view is spectacular.
🚌 Travel~1.5–2 hrs each way by tour bus. Full tour ~6–7 hrs total.

Hanoi
15–23 June · 7 nights · Old Quarter (TBC)
06
Old Quarter & Hoan Kiem Lake
Tuesday 16 June
HistoryFoodShopping
▼
  • Slow start — After the late arrival, take it easy. Hotel breakfast and a gentle morning coffee.
  • 9:00 AM — Hoa Lo Prison ("Hanoi Hilton"). Fascinating history — audio guide highly recommended. ~1.5 hrs.
  • 11:00 AM — Walk to Hoan Kiem Lake (~10 mins). Beautiful flat lake walk. Visit Ngoc Son Temple on the island — red bridge, great photos.
  • Afternoon — Wander the Old Quarter's 36 ancient streets. Each traditionally sold one trade — silk, paper, tin, herbs.
  • Evening — Egg coffee (Cafe Giang, Hang Gai — the original), Banh Mi, fresh spring rolls.
💡 Egg CoffeeCà phê trúng is a Hanoi invention — creamy, rich, extraordinary. Don't leave without trying it.

NatureFoodRelaxed

Be Prepared

Packing & Tips

Having done a fair bit of travel in Southeast Asia, these are the things that genuinely matter. Don't skip the mosquito repellent — you will regret it.

👕
Clothing
  • Light breathable fabrics only — linen, cotton. Not synthetics in this heat.
  • Modest cover-ups for temples (shoulders & knees covered).
  • Rain jacket or packable poncho — June is early wet season.
  • Comfortable walking sandals + sneakers.
  • Swimwear for the rooftop pool and Ha Long Bay.
  • Light layer for air-conditioned buses and restaurants — they crank the AC.
💊
Health
  • See a doctor 6–8 weeks before — Hep A, Typhoid, Tetanus.
  • DEET mosquito repellent 50%+ — non-negotiable.
  • Sunscreen SPF50+ and reapply constantly.
  • Rehydration sachets — the humidity is serious.
  • Basic first aid kit + all personal medications.
  • Travel insurance with medical cover. Don't skip this.
📱
Tech & Money
  • Download Grab app before you arrive.
  • Get a local SIM at the airport — cheap and fast.
  • Notify your bank before travel.
  • VND cash for markets and street food.
  • Portable phone charger — full days drain batteries fast.
  • Universal adaptor — Vietnam uses Type A/C outlets.
🏈
Day Trips
  • Small daypack for excursions.
  • Reusable water bottle — stay hydrated constantly.
  • Wet wipes and hand sanitiser.
  • Hat or cap for outdoor sites.
  • Waterproof bag for Ha Long Bay.
  • Snacks for the long bus transfers.

Essential Tips

🇦🇺 Visa

Australians get 45 days visa-free into Vietnam right now. No visa needed — just make sure your passport is valid for 6+ months from your travel date.

🌧️ June Weather

June is early wet season — expect afternoon showers, especially in the south. Temperatures 28–35°C. Rain passes quickly. Pack a poncho and embrace it.

🚖 Getting Around

Grab for all taxis — safe, reliable, metered. Never use unofficial airport taxis. Book tours through Klook or GetYourGuide for good reviews.

🍜 Food Safety

Street food is safe and extraordinary — eat where locals eat. Avoid ice unless at a proper restaurant. Bottled water everywhere, never tap water.

📅 Book Ahead

Ha Long Bay cruises sell out in June — book as soon as possible. Cu Chi and Mekong tours can wait until 1–2 days before.

💰 Budget Guide

Street meals: ~30–80k VND. Restaurant mains: ~100–200k VND. Grab rides: ~50–150k VND. Budget ~AUD $80–120/day per person all in.
