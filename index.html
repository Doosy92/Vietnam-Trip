2
History & Markets
Friday 12 June
HistoryShoppingFood
▼
  • 7:30 AM — War Remnants Museum, and we go early before the heat hits. It's genuinely one of the most important things to see in Vietnam — heavy going emotionally but unmissable. There's a lift throughout. Allow 2–3 hours and take your time with it.
  • ~11 AM — Back to the hotel. Lunch, pool, rest. This is the routine we'll get into — mornings for sightseeing, avoid the 12–2 PM furnace.
  • 2:00 PM — Ben Thanh Market. Touristy but fun — good for souvenirs, Vietnamese coffee, silk scarves, spices, snacks. Prices start high so haggling is expected. My tip: start at around 25–30% of what they ask and meet somewhere in the middle.
  • Evening — The market area turns into a street food night market after 6 PM. Good vibe, cheap beers, great food.
🚖 Travel: Hotel to War Remnants Museum is literally a 5-min Grab — practically around the corner from where we're staying. Ben Thanh is another 10 min from there.
💡 Haggling: Be cheerful about it, it's supposed to be fun. If they won't come down in price and you think it's too much — just start slowly walking away. Works every single time.
3
Cu Chi Tunnels Day Trip
Saturday 13 June
HistoryOutdoors
▼
  • 7:30–8:00 AM — Tour bus picks us up from the hotel. I'll book this in advance — going for a small group tour rather than the giant 40-person buses.
  • About 1.5–2 hour drive northwest out of the city. Gets more interesting the further out you go.
  • They start with an air-conditioned documentary screening — fascinating context and a comfortable sit-down for everyone.
  • Outdoor exhibits at your own pace: jungle booby traps, bomb craters, old military equipment. Lots of shade and benches throughout.
  • Jason — you can crawl through the actual tunnels. I'd recommend the enlarged tourist version over the original sized ones unless you really want to test yourself 😂
  • 3–4 PM — Back at the hotel. Rooftop pool. Rest. Earnt.
🚌 Travel time: ~1.5–2 hrs each way by coach. Full day out is roughly 6–7 hours total.
💡 Good to know: The site is completely at your own pace — benches and shade throughout, no pressure to rush anything. The tunnels themselves are optional, there's plenty to see above ground.
4
Mekong Delta — Full Day Boat Tour
Sunday 14 June
NatureFoodRelaxed
▼
  • 7:30–8:00 AM — Pickup from hotel.
  • ~1.5–2 hr drive south to the river delta. Good opportunity to nap on the bus if needed.
  • Onto the boat — we cruise through the canals and islands. Rice paddies, coconut palms, small fishing villages. It's a completely different side of Vietnam.
  • Stops along the way: a coconut candy factory (Mum, this one's for you), bee farm with honey tea tasting, tropical fruit garden where locals play traditional music while you eat fresh fruit — it's as lovely as it sounds.
  • A narrow canal canoe ride where local rowers take you through the jungle. You sit back and enjoy it — one of the most relaxed things we'll do on the whole trip.
  • Lunch is included on the tour.
  • 5–6 PM — Back in Saigon. Last night in HCMC — let's find somewhere good for dinner and make the most of it.
🚌 Travel time: ~1.5–2 hrs each way. Full day is 8–10 hours but it's almost entirely just sitting on boats and buses — very easy going.

6
Old Quarter, Hoan Kiem Lake & Hoa Lo Prison
Tuesday 16 June
HistoryFoodShopping
▼
  • Slow morning — We arrived late last night so no rushing. Breakfast, coffee, get our bearings. Hanoi feels completely different to HCMC — older, cooler in temperature and atmosphere, more French colonial architecture.
  • 9:00 AM — Hoa Lo Prison (nicknamed the "Hanoi Hilton" by American POWs, which tells you something). Covers both the French colonial era AND the Vietnam War. Get the audio guide — it's worth every penny. About 1.5 hours.
  • 11:00 AM — Walk down to Hoan Kiem Lake (10 mins from the prison). Beautiful flat lake circuit — there's a little temple on an island in the middle connected by a red bridge that's incredibly photogenic.
  • Afternoon — Wander the 36 streets of the Old Quarter. Each street historically sold one specific trade — silk on one, tin on another, paper on the next. Great for browsing even if you're not buying.
  • Evening — Street food walk. Try egg coffee if you haven't yet (more on that in the tips section — trust me on this one).
🚶 Getting around: Pretty much everything today is walkable from the hotel. Flat paths all the way around the lake.
7–8
Ha Long Bay — Overnight Cruise ⭐ The Big One
Wednesday–Thursday 17–18 June
NatureFoodUnmissable
▼
  • This is the one I'm most excited about. Ha Long Bay is genuinely one of the most spectacular places I've ever been — thousands of limestone towers rising out of emerald green water. You can't come to Vietnam and not do this.
  • Day 7, 8:00 AM — Coach picks us up from the hotel. 3.5 hr drive through the Vietnamese countryside to the bay.
  • ~12:00 PM — Board the cruise. Welcome lunch on deck as we sail out into the bay. The moment the limestone karsts appear around you, everyone goes quiet. It does that to people.
  • Afternoon: cruising through the islands, visiting Sung Sot Cave (bring your phone torch), optional kayaking around the karsts.
  • Sunset on deck with drinks. Proper seafood dinner on board. Sleep on the boat — the gentle rocking is actually brilliant for sleeping.
  • Day 8, morning — Sunrise on deck. More cruising, breakfast on board. Disembark around midday.
  • ~3:30–4 PM — Coach back to Hanoi. Hotel by about 7:30 PM. Early night — two big days.
🚌 Travel: 3.5 hrs each way. I'm booking this one straight away — good cabins in June fill up fast. Looking at a mid-range boat with proper rooms for the four of us.
💡 The best part: This is genuinely one of the most relaxed days on the whole trip. You're on a boat, there's a deck to sit on, food comes to you, and the views are doing all the work. Kayaking is completely optional — just skip it if you'd rather not.
9
Temple of Literature & Shopping
Friday 19 June
CultureShoppingEasy
▼
  • Slow start — We'll need it after two days on the bay. Proper breakfast, no alarm, no rushing.
  • 9:30 AM — Temple of Literature. Vietnam's first ever university, built in 1070. The courtyards are genuinely beautiful and it's a really peaceful spot in the middle of the city. Hire a guide when you get there — the history is fascinating but without one you're really just looking at old courtyards. About 1.5 hours.
  • Afternoon — Shopping in the Old Quarter. Hang Gai Street is where you go for silk, lacquerware and custom tailoring. Dong Xuan Market for a proper local market atmosphere. A lot of shops can do custom clothing in 24 hours if anyone wants something made.
  • Evening — Dinner. Try Cha Ca La Vong — turmeric fish cooked at the table with dill. Sounds odd, genuinely brilliant.
🚖 Travel: Hotel to Temple of Literature is ~15–20 min Grab each way. Easy.
10
Ninh Binh — "Ha Long Bay on Land"
Saturday 20 June
NatureHistoryScenic
▼
  • 7:00 AM — Early start. I know. Worth it.
  • ~2.5 hour drive south. The countryside opens up beautifully once you're clear of Hanoi.
  • Ninh Binh is called the "inland Ha Long Bay" and that's exactly what it looks like — the same dramatic limestone karsts, but surrounded by rice paddies and winding rivers instead of sea. It's stunning in a completely different way.
  • Trang An: we get into rowboats and local rowers take us through caves, past ancient temples, and under towering limestone cliffs. You literally just sit there and take it in — genuinely one of the most peaceful things I've ever done.
  • Hoa Lu: Vietnam's ancient royal capital. Temples set right into the base of the mountains.
  • Lunch in Ninh Binh town. The local speciality is goat meat — don't knock it until you've tried it.
  • ~3:30 PM — Head back.
  • ~6:00 PM — Back in Hanoi. Quiet evening.
🚌 Travel: ~2.5 hrs each way. Long day but genuinely worth every minute. I'll book a guided tour with hotel pickup.

Chill Day & Street Food Tour
Sunday 21 June
FoodRelaxed
▼
  • Morning — Coffee by Hoan Kiem Lake. Locals do tai chi here in the mornings and it's a genuinely lovely atmosphere. Just sit, watch the city wake up, drink excellent Vietnamese coffee.
  • Afternoon — Free time. Mum, I'd strongly recommend a proper spa afternoon — Vietnamese massage and reflexology is exceptional and ridiculously affordable. Jason can find his own trouble 😄

Read This Before You Pack

Packing & Tips

A few things I'd want to know before heading over. Some of these I learned the hard way so you don't have to.

👕
What to Wear
  • Light, breathable fabrics only — linen and loose cotton. Anything synthetic and you'll be miserable in the humidity.
  • Bring a thin cardigan or layer — restaurants and tour buses are absolutely blasted with aircon and you'll go from furnace to freezer constantly.
  • A couple of modest outfits for temples (shoulders and knees covered). You can buy cheap cover-ups at the markets once we're there.
  • Comfortable shoes you can genuinely walk in. And sandals for evenings.
  • Swimwear — rooftop pool in HCMC and Ha Long Bay boat deck.
  • A packable rain jacket. June is rainy season. The rain is warm and passes fast but you'll be glad you have it.
💊
Health Bits
  • See your GP at least 6 weeks before — worth getting Hep A, Typhoid and Tetanus checked. I'm doing this regardless.
  • Mosquito repellent — get the DEET-based stuff, 50%+ strength. The weak supermarket versions aren't enough.
  • Sunscreen SPF50+ and reapply it constantly. The June sun in Vietnam is no joke.
  • Rehydration sachets. The humidity is intense and you'll sweat more than you realise.
  • All personal medications — bring more than you think you'll need.
  • Travel insurance with proper medical cover. Non-negotiable for all of us.
📱
Tech & Money
  • Download Grab before we leave and get it set up with payment. This is how we get everywhere — I'll help everyone set it up.
  • Get a local SIM at the airport when we land. Cheap, fast data, much better than paying roaming fees.
  • Tell your bank you're travelling before you go or your card will get frozen the first time you use it overseas.
  • Carry some VND cash for markets and street food — lots of small vendors don't take cards.
  • Portable power bank. Your phone will die faster than usual with Grab and maps running constantly.
  • Vietnam uses Type A and C plugs — bring a universal adaptor.
🎒
Day Trip Bag
  • A small daypack or crossbody bag for excursions — not your big suitcase.
  • Reusable water bottle. Fill it up constantly — staying hydrated in that heat matters.
  • Wet wipes and hand sanitiser. More useful than you'd think, especially at street food spots.
  • Hat or cap for anything with outdoor walking.
  • A small waterproof bag for Ha Long Bay — the boat trips can get splashy.
  • Snacks for the long coach transfers (Cu Chi and Ninh Binh are both 2+ hrs each way).

Things Worth Knowing

🇦🇺 Visa — No Stress

Australians get 45 days visa-free entry. No forms, no fees, no stress. Just rock up with a valid passport. Make sure yours is valid for at least 6 months past June 2026 and you're sorted.

🌧️ June Weather — It'll Be Hot

Hot, humid, and occasional afternoon downpours. We're talking 30–35°C most days. The rain usually comes and goes quickly and is actually a relief when it does. Pack the poncho and don't let it ruin the vibe — the locals don't.

🚖 Getting Around — Just Use Grab

Grab is Uber but better. Safe, air-conditioned, metered, reliable. Use it for everything. Never get into an unofficial taxi especially at airports. I'll make sure everyone has the app set up and working before we land.

🍜 The Food — Get Involved

The street food is genuinely incredible and largely safe. Eat where locals eat. Avoid ice in drinks at sketchy spots. Don't drink tap water — bottled water is everywhere and costs almost nothing. The food alone is worth the trip, honestly.

📅 Ha Long Bay — Booking Early

I'm booking Ha Long Bay as soon as this site goes up. June is peak season and the decent boats fill up fast. Everything else — Cu Chi, Mekong Delta, Ninh Binh — can be sorted a few days before through Klook or GetYourGuide. I'll handle all the bookings.

💰 What It'll Cost

Vietnam is great value. Street meals: roughly AUD $2–5. A proper restaurant dinner: $10–20 per person. Grab rides around the city: $2–5. I'd budget around AUD $80–100 per person per day for spending money on top of accommodation — and that's being comfortable.
